# Orthogonal Projections
## Inner Product, Length, and Orthogonality
### Dot Products and Length
#### Dot Product
The dot product between two vectors, $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as following.
$$
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}
= \vec{u}^{\,T}\vec{v} \
= (u_1\; u_2\; \cdots\; u_n)
\begin{pmatrix}
v_1 \\
v_2 \\
\vdots \\
v_n
\end{pmatrix}
= u_1 v_1 + u_2 v_2 + \cdots + u_n v_n
$$

#### Theorem: Dot Product Identities
Let $\vec{u}$; $\vec{v}$, $\vec{w}$  be three vectors in $\mathbb{R}^n$, and $c \in \mathbb{R}$.
- $(\vec{v} + \vec{w}) \cdot \vec{u} = \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} + \vec{w} \cdot \vec{u}$ (linearity)
- $(c\vec{u}) \cdot \vec{w} = c(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{w})$ (scalars)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u}$ (symmetry)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u} \ge 0$ (positivity)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u} = 0 \iff \vec{u} = \vec{0}$ 

#### Length of Vector
The length of a vector $\vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as following.
$$
\|\vec{u}\|
= \sqrt{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u}}
= \sqrt{u_1^2 + u_2^2 + \cdots + u_n^2}
$$

For any vector $\vec{v}$ and scalar $c$, the length of $c \vec{v}$ can be represented as following.
$$
\|c\vec{v}\| = |c| \, \|\vec{v}\|
$$
Note that $|c|$ is absolute value of $c$.

#### Unit Vector
If $\vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ has length one, we say that it is a unit vector. For example,each of the following vectors are unit vectors.
$$
\vec{e}_1 = \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}, \quad
\vec{y} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\begin{pmatrix}1\\2\end{pmatrix}, \quad
\vec{v} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\1\\1\end{pmatrix}
$$

#### Distance in $\mathbb{R}^n$
For $ \vec{u}, \vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$, the distance between $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ is given by the formula $\|\vec{u} - \vec{v} \|$.

#### Angle
$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \|\vec{a}\| \, \|\vec{b}\| \cos \theta$$
Thus, if $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0$ then, $\vec{a}$ and/or $\vec{b}$ are zero vectors, or $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ are perpendicular to each other.

### Orthogonality
Two vectors $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ are orthogonal if $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0$. This is equivalent to following equation.
$$
\| \vec{u} + \vec{v} \| = \| \vec{u} \|^2 + \| \vec{v} \|^2 \\[5pt]

\|\vec{u} + \vec{v}\|^2
= (\vec{u} + \vec{v}) \cdot (\vec{u} + \vec{v})
= \vec{u}\cdot\vec{u} + \vec{v}\cdot\vec{v} + 2\,\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}
= \|\vec{u}\|^2 + \|\vec{v}\|^2 + 2\,\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}
$$

Note that if two vectors are orthogonal, it is equivalent to $n$-dimensional version of the Pythagorean Theorem. Also note that the zero vector is orthogonal to every vector, but usually only mean non-zero vectors when discussing orthogonality.

### Orthogonal Compliments
Let $W$ be a subspace of $\mathbb{R}^$n$. Vector $\vec{Z} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is orthogonal to $W$ if $\vec{z}$ is orthogonal to every vector in $W$.  

The set of all vectors orthogonal to $W$ is a subspace, the orthogonal compliment of $W$, or $W^\perp$.
$$
W^\perp = \left\{ \vec{z} \in \mathbb{R}^n \;:\; \vec{z} \cdot \vec{w} = 0 \;\text{for all } \vec{w} \in W \right\}
$$

For example,
$$
A = \begin{pmatrix}
1 & 3 \\
2 & 6
\end{pmatrix} \\[5pt]
\operatorname{Col}(A) = \operatorname{span}\left\{ \vec{a}_1 = \begin{pmatrix}1 \\ 2\end{pmatrix} \right\}, \quad
(\operatorname{Col}(A))^\perp = \operatorname{span}\left\{ \vec{z} = \begin{pmatrix}2 \\ -1\end{pmatrix} \right\}
$$

### The Four Fundamental Subspaces
#### Row A
Row A is the space spanned by the rows of matrix $A$.  
- A basis for Row A is given by the pivot rows of $A$.
- dim(Row A) = dim(Col A) (number of column pivots = number of row pivots)
- Row A = Col A ^T

즉, pivot 개수 = rank = row space dimension = column space dimension.

### Relationships Between ColA and NulA^T
For any $A \in $R^{m \times n}$, the orthogonal complement of Row A is Nul A,
and the orthogonal complement of Col A is Nul $A^T$.

$$
A^T \vec{x} = \vec{0}
$$
- $\vec{x}$ is orthogonal to the rows of $A^T$.
- $\vec{x}$ is orthogonal to the columns of $A$
- Col $A$ is orthogonal to Nul $A^T$, in other words, Col $A^\perp$ = Nul $A^T$.
