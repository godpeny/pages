## Inner Product, Length, and Orthogonality
### Dot Products and Length
#### Dot Product
The dot product between two vectors, $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as following.
$$
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v}
= \vec{u}^{\,T}\vec{v} \
= (u_1\; u_2\; \cdots\; u_n)
\begin{pmatrix}
v_1 \\
v_2 \\
\vdots \\
v_n
\end{pmatrix}
= u_1 v_1 + u_2 v_2 + \cdots + u_n v_n
$$

#### Theorem: Dot Product Identities
Let $\vec{u}$; $\vec{v}$, $\vec{w}$  be three vectors in $\mathbb{R}^n$, and $c \in \mathbb{R}$.
- $(\vec{v} + \vec{w}) \cdot \vec{u} = \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u} + \vec{w} \cdot \vec{u}$ (linearity)
- $(c\vec{u}) \cdot \vec{w} = c(\vec{u} \cdot \vec{w})$ (scalars)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = \vec{v} \cdot \vec{u}$ (symmetry)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u} \ge 0$ (positivity)
- $\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u} = 0 \iff \vec{u} = \vec{0}$ 

#### Length of Vector
The length of a vector $\vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ is defined as following.
$$
\|\vec{u}\|
= \sqrt{\vec{u} \cdot \vec{u}}
= \sqrt{u_1^2 + u_2^2 + \cdots + u_n^2}
$$

For any vector $\vec{v}$ and scalar $c$, the length of $c \vec{v}$ can be represented as following.
$$
\|c\vec{v}\| = |c| \, \|\vec{v}\|
$$
Note that $|c|$ is absolute value of $c$.

#### Unit Vector
If $\vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$ has length one, we say that it is a unit vector. For example,each of the following vectors are unit vectors.
$$
\vec{e}_1 = \begin{pmatrix}1\\0\end{pmatrix}, \quad
\vec{y} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}}\begin{pmatrix}1\\2\end{pmatrix}, \quad
\vec{v} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}}\begin{pmatrix}1\\0\\1\\1\end{pmatrix}
$$

#### Distance in $\mathbb{R}^n$
For $ \vec{u}, \vec{v} \in \mathbb{R}^n$, the distance between $\vec{u}$ and $\vec{v}$ is given by the formula $\|\vec{u} - \vec{v} \|$.

#### Angle
$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = \|\vec{a}\| \, \|\vec{b}\| \cos \theta$$
Thus, if $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0$ then, $\vec{a}$ and/or $\vec{b}$ are zero vectors, or $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$ are perpendicular to each other.
